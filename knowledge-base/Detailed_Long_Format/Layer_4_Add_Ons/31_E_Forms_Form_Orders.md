# E-Forms: Form Orders

Form Orders replace static PDF filling with interactive, intelligent web forms that capture structured data, generate a clean PDF, and feed the signer straight into the signing ceremony - with every field value flowing automatically to your downstream systems as structured metadata.

- **End-to-end workflow** - your employee selects a pre-configured form in the portal, enters the recipient's details, and dispatches a private, secure link via email or SMS. The recipient clicks the link, authenticates (via SMS OTP or strong bank authentication depending on your configuration), and is presented with a responsive web form. Upon submission, the platform's conversion engine merges the form data (JSON) into an empty PDF template in real-time, generating a fully populated document. The recipient is then seamlessly redirected to the signing ceremony to execute their signature - no email wait, no manual steps

- **Cascading logic** - form fields dynamically expand, appear, or hide based on previous answers. If a user selects "Yes" to a pre-existing condition, new mandatory fields appear immediately. This allows complex questionnaires - some containing over 180 fields - to be condensed into an intuitive, guided experience that only shows what is relevant

- **Metadata extraction to downstream systems** - every value the signer enters is automatically extracted as structured key-value metadata. When signing completes, your CRM or ERP receives the webhook, pulls the metadata via API, and routes it directly into the correct customer record. No employee needs to open the PDF and re-type the answers. Fennia Insurance achieves zero manual data entry through this pipeline

- **What this replaces** - standard fillable PDFs lack complex validation, display poorly on mobile devices, and require employees to manually extract data afterward. Form Orders provide validated, structured field capture at the point of signing with enforced input rules (character limits, email format validation) that prevent dirty or incomplete submissions

- **Real use cases** - Fennia Insurance processes complex applications where form data flows directly to their CRM and archiving backend. Aktia Bank uses cascading logic forms for financial product applications where disclosures change based on customer selections
