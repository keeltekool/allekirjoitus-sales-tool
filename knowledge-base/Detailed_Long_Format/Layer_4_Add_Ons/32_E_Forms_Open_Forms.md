# E-Forms: Open Forms

Open Forms are public-facing, self-service versions of Form Orders - embedded directly on your website with a unique URL, requiring no employee to dispatch an invitation. Your customers or applicants land on the form, authenticate, fill in their data, and sign in one uninterrupted flow.

- **How they differ from Form Orders** - Form Orders are private workflows: an employee manually selects a form, enters the recipient, and dispatches a link. Open Forms are entirely public and self-serve. Each form gets a unique public URL that you embed on your website (e.g., behind an "Apply Now" button). Because the link is publicly accessible, no invitation email or manual dispatch by your staff is needed

- **Mandatory strong authentication** - because the form is public and accessible to anyone on the internet, the very first step after clicking the link is mandatory strong authentication (via bank ID or Mobile Certificate). This ensures the identity of the anonymous visitor is verified before any sensitive data is collected or a document is generated

- **The visitor journey** - the flow is continuous and frictionless: (1) the visitor clicks the embedded link on your website, (2) they are immediately prompted for strong identity verification, (3) once authenticated, the interactive form is presented, (4) upon submission, the platform generates the PDF in the background and seamlessly redirects the visitor straight to the signing ceremony. They do not close their browser or wait for an email - the session continues uninterrupted through data entry to signature execution

- **Embedding on your website** - to ensure the transition from your website to the form feels native, the form and signing ceremony can be hosted on a branded custom domain (e.g., allekirjoitus.kuntarahoitus.fi), making the entire experience appear as part of your own digital service

- **Use cases** - loan applications on bank websites (Kuntarahoitus uses Open Forms for 24/7 municipal loan applications), insurance claim and quote submissions, self-service customer onboarding where new users register, provide legal details, and sign terms in one continuous web flow
