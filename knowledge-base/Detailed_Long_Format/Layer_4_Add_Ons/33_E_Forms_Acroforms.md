# E-Forms: Acroforms

Acroforms let your signers fill in standard PDF form fields directly during the signing ceremony - no custom development, no monthly form fees, and no engineering work required. If you already have fillable PDFs, they work as-is.

- **How Acroforms work** - when you attach a pre-existing fillable PDF (an Acroform) to a signature request, the signer sees the fillable document within the signing interface. They complete the required fields and execute their signature in one continuous session. The platform processes the standard PDF form fields natively

- **No development needed** - unlike Form Orders and Open Forms, which require the development team to build custom HTML forms with CSS styling and validation logic, Acroforms use the fillable PDFs your organization already has. There is no custom engineering, no form creation project, and no additional monthly fee - Acroforms are included in the base transaction cost

- **When to use Acroforms vs. Form Orders vs. Open Forms** - use Acroforms for quick deployment, simple data collection, and low-volume occasional use where you have a basic single-signer PDF that needs filling. Use Form Orders when you need complex workflows with conditional logic, strict field validation, multiple signatories, and automatic metadata extraction to downstream systems. Use Open Forms when you need a public-facing, self-service form embedded on your website for unknown applicants

- **Limitations** - Acroforms currently support only single-signatory requests. They lack advanced capabilities like cascading conditional fields, custom validation rules, and automatic metadata extraction to CRM or archive systems. For simple, straightforward forms these limitations rarely matter; for complex enterprise workflows with deep system integration requirements, Form Orders are the appropriate choice

- **Advantages** - zero setup time, zero development cost, works with existing PDF forms, included in base pricing, immediate deployment for any single-signer fillable document workflow
