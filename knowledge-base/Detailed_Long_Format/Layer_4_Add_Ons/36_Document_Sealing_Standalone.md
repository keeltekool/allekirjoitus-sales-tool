# Document Sealing (Standalone)

Standalone document sealing applies your organization's official digital seal to PDF documents without any human signatory - the digital equivalent of a company stamp. Every sealed document receives a qualified timestamp proving integrity and origin, making it suitable for automated, high-volume document production.

- **What standalone sealing is** - while a standard electronic signature authenticates an individual person, an electronic seal authenticates the legal entity itself. Your organization's digital certificate is applied to the document, proving it originated from your organization and has not been tampered with. No person is invited to a signing ceremony, no authentication flow occurs, and no signatory data or XAdES audit trail is embedded

- **How it differs from signing** - in a standard signing workflow, human signers authenticate, the system records personal audit data (identity, IP, timestamps) in XAdES XML files, and these are embedded in the final document. Standalone sealing bypasses all human involvement. The platform routes the PDF directly to the signing engine, which applies the organizational certificate and qualified timestamp. The sealed document contains no signatory data and no XAdES attachments - only the organizational seal and timestamp

- **API-driven bulk sealing** - sealing is optimized for zero-touch, API-driven workflows. Your billing system, HR platform, or document management system pushes documents directly to the sealing API. Dedicated processing endpoints handle sealing traffic at scale, enabling fully automated pipelines that process thousands of documents without human intervention

- **Qualified timestamp** - every sealed document automatically receives an eIDAS-qualified timestamp, providing mathematically verifiable proof of the exact moment the seal was applied and guaranteeing the document has not been altered since

- **Use cases** - automated sealing of monthly invoices, payslips, and salary certificates as they are generated by financial and HR systems. Authenticating official company correspondence, payment confirmations, and legal notices. Government agencies and municipalities sealing official decisions, permits, and certificates issued to citizens at volume
